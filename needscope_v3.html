How We Ranked Every Problem
We didn't just count complaints — we scored each unmet need on three dimensions, because not all complaints matter equally.
40%
How Often It's Mentioned
A problem appearing in 600 reviews matters more than one in 50. Frequency tells us how widespread the pain is across the market.
611 reviews mention ingredient transparency
35%
How Angry Customers Are
Complaints from 1-star reviews signal a product failure. We weight deeper frustration higher — a 1★ complaint carries 5× more than a 4★ one.
Avg 2.1★ = extreme frustration signal
25%
How Many People Agree
Helpful votes confirm other shoppers felt the same pain. One angry person is noise. Five hundred agreeing? That's a market signal.
Avg 50 helpful votes per complaint
Opportunity Score = F(0.40) + S(0.35) + V(0.25)
F = frequency_count / max_frequency ← normalised 0–1
S = (6 − avg_rating) / 5 ← severity, inverted scale
V = avg_helpful_votes / max_helpful_votes ← social validation
Final score mapped to 50–100 range using power normalisation (exponent 0.6) to spread ranks meaningfully.

Analyst's Note — Early Signals
Western Trends Heading to India
Beyond what the data shows — emerging consumer behaviours in the West that typically arrive in India 2–3 years later. Not data-backed, but market-aware.
🧬
Skin Microbiome Care
Probiotic-based skincare is growing rapidly in the US and EU — targeting the skin's bacterial ecosystem, not just surface symptoms. Indian consumers are beginning to encounter this language.
Early Signal — US/EU
🧪
Clinical-Grade Personalisation
Brands like Curology and Function of Beauty have normalised quiz-based formulation in the West. Our data confirms personalisation_demand is already top-5 in India — the gap is validated.
Data-Confirmed Crossover
♻️
Refill & Zero-Waste Models
Sustainable packaging is a growing pain point in our data. In the West, refillable deodorants and serum pods are mainstream. India's urban consumer is ready — the infrastructure isn't yet.
Early Signal — UK/EU
